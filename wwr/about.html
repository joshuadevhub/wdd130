History

White water rafting began as a practical means for early explorers and traders to navigate rivers and transport goods through rough waters. In the mid-20th century, it evolved into a popular recreational activity when adventurers sought thrilling ways to experience nature

The sport gained worldwide attention in the 1960s, thanks to pioneers who developed specialized rafts and safety equipment. Today, white water rafting is enjoyed by millions, offering everything from gentle river floats to extreme rapids, combining excitement with stunning outdoor scenery.
